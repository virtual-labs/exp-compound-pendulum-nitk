Double Pendulum

A double pendulum system as shown in Fig 1, consists of two-point masses m1m_1m1​ and m2m_2m2​ suspended by strings of length l1l_1l1​ and l2l_2l2​ as shown below. Let the system vibrates in vertical plane with small amplitudes, under which conditions the masses may be considered to have only horizontal motion. Let θ1θ_1θ1​ and θ2θ_2θ2​ be the angles the strings at any instant make with vertical and let x1x_1x1​ and x2x_2x2​ be the horizontal displacements of the two masses, such that the following relationships holds good for small amplitudes.

sin⁡θ1=θ1=x1l1\sin \theta_1 = \theta_1 = \frac{x_1}{l_1}sinθ1​=θ1​=l1​x1​​
sin⁡θ1=θ2=x2−x1l2\sin \theta_1 = \theta_2 = \frac{x_2-x_1}{l_2}sinθ1​=θ2​=l2​x2​−x1​​

For finding natural frequency and mode shapes: Considering no motion in vertical direction, the vertical components of forces on each of two masses must balance. Therefore,

T2=m2gT_2 = m_2g T2​=m2​g
T1=m1g+T2=(m1−m2)gT_1 = m_1g + T_2 = (m_1 - m_2)gT1​=m1​g+T2​=(m1​−m2​)g

Fig 2. Free body diagram of the double pendulum

Writing down the equation of motion of two masses for motion in horizontal direction from Fig 2, we have

m1x¨1=T2sin⁡θ2−T1sin⁡θ1m_1\ddot{x}_1 = T_2 \sin\theta_2 - T_1 \sin\theta_1m1​x¨1​=T2​sinθ2​−T1​sinθ1​
m2x¨2=−T2sin⁡θ2m_2\ddot{x}_2 = -T_2 \sin\theta_2m2​x¨2​=−T2​sinθ2​

If we take T1T_1T1​ and T2T_2T2​ values in the above equation, we get

m1x¨1+[m1+m2l1+m2l2]gx1=m2l2gx2m_1\ddot{x}_1 + \left[ \frac{m_1+m_2}{l_1} + \frac{m_2}{l_2} \right] gx_1 = \frac{m_2}{l_2} gx_2 m1​x¨1​+[l1​m1​+m2​​+l2​m2​​]gx1​=l2​m2​​gx2​
m2x¨2+m2l2gx2=m2l2gx1m_2\ddot{x}_2 + \frac{m_2}{l_2} gx_2 = \frac{m_2}{l_2} gx_1m2​x¨2​+l2​m2​​gx2​=l2​m2​​gx1​

Assuming the general solution of equation of motion to be:

x1=X1sin⁡(ωt)x_1 = X_1 \sin(\omega t)x1​=X1​sin(ωt)
x2=X2sin⁡(ωt)x_2 = X_2 \sin(\omega t)x2​=X2​sin(ωt)

Substituting the above solutions with the equation of motion, we get

{−m1ω2+[m1+m2l1+m2l2]g}X1=m2l2gX2\left\{ -m_1\omega^2 + \left[ \frac{m_1+m_2}{l_1} + \frac{m_2}{l_2} \right]g \right\} X_1 = \frac{m_2}{l_2} g X_2{−m1​ω2+[l1​m1​+m2​​+l2​m2​​]g}X1​=l2​m2​​gX2​
{−m2ω2+[m2l2]g}X2=m2l2gX1\left\{ -m_2\omega^2 + \left[ \frac{m_2}{l_2} \right]g \right\} X_2 = \frac{m_2}{l_2} g X_1{−m2​ω2+[l2​m2​​]g}X2​=l2​m2​​gX1​

This equation will give,

X1X2=m2l2g−m1ω2+[m1+m2l1+m2l2]g\frac{X_1}{X_2} = \frac{\frac{m_2}{l_2}g}{-m_1\omega^2+\left[\frac{m_1+m_2}{l_1}+\frac{m_2}{l_2}\right]g}X2​X1​​=−m1​ω2+[l1​m1​+m2​​+l2​m2​​]gl2​m2​​g​
X1X2=−m2ω2+[m2l2]gm2l2gX1\frac{X_1}{X_2} = \frac{-m_2\omega^2+\left[\frac{m_2}{l_2}\right]g}{\frac{m_2}{l_2}gX_1}X2​X1​​=l2​m2​​gX1​−m2​ω2+[l2​m2​​]g​

Equating the two expressions in the above two equations and cross multiplying.

If E=(m2g)/l2E = (m_2 g)/l_2E=(m2​g)/l2​ and F=(m1+m2)g/l1F = (m_1+m_2)g/l_1F=(m1​+m2​)g/l1​ , then the frequency equation is

ω4−[(m1+m2)E+m2Fm1m2]ω2+FEm1m2=0\omega^4 - [\frac{(m_1+m_2)E + m_2F}{m_1m_2}]\omega^2 + \frac{FE}{m_1m_2} = 0ω4−[m1​m2​(m1​+m2​)E+m2​F​]ω2+m1​m2​FE​=0

The above equation is quadratic in ω2\omega^2ω2 and gives two values of ω2\omega^2ω2, and therefore two positive values of ω\omegaω corresponding to two natural frequencies ωn1\omega_{n1}ωn1​ and ωn2\omega_{n2}ωn2​ of the system. The above equation is called the frequency equation since the roots of this equation gives the natural frequencies of the system. Considering,

m1=m2=mm_1 = m_2 = mm1​=m2​=m
l1=l2=ll_1 = l_2 = ll1​=l2​=l

The above equations become,

X1X2=g/l3gl−ω2\frac{X_1}{X_2} = \frac{g/l}{\frac{3g}{l} - \omega^2}X2​X1​​=l3g​−ω2g/l​

X1X2=gl−ω2gl\frac{X_1}{X_2} = \frac{\frac{g}{l} - \omega^2}{\frac{g}{l}}X2​X1​​=lg​lg​−ω2​

which gives,

g/l3gl−ω2=gl−ω2gl\frac{g/l}{\frac{3g}{l} - \omega^2}=\frac{\frac{g}{l} - \omega^2}{\frac{g}{l}}l3g​−ω2g/l​=lg​lg​−ω2​

or

w4g2−4w4lg+2l2=0\frac{w^4}{g^2} - \frac{4w^4}{l^g} + \frac{2}{l^2} = 0g2w4​−lg4w4​+l22​=0

which is the frequency equation.

Solving for ω\omegaω, using quadratic equation we have the two values of the natural frequencies as,

wn1=gl(2−2)w_{n1} = \sqrt{\frac{g}{l}(2-\sqrt2)}wn1​=lg​(2−2​)​
wn2=gl(2+2)w_{n2} = \sqrt{\frac{g}{l}(2+\sqrt2)}wn2​=lg​(2+2​)​

The amplitude ratio of the equations are:

X1X2=m2l2g−m1ω2+[m1+m2l1+m2l2]g\frac{X_1}{X_2} = \frac{\frac{m_2}{l_2} g}{-m_1 \omega^2 + \left[ \frac{m_1 + m_2}{l_1} + \frac{m_2}{l_2} \right] g}X2​X1​​=−m1​ω2+[l1​m1​+m2​​+l2​m2​​]gl2​m2​​g​

X1X2=−m2ω2+[m2l2]gm2l2gX1\frac{X_1}{X_2} = \frac{ -m_2 \omega^2 + \left[ \frac{m_2}{l_2} \right] g }{ \frac{m_2}{l_2} g X_1 }X2​X1​​=l2​m2​​gX1​−m2​ω2+[l2​m2​​]g​

If in either of the equations, on substituting for ω\omegaω the value of ωn1\omega_{n1}ωn1​, we have the first mode shape of the system i.e., when the system is vibrating with the first natural frequency ωn1\omega_{n1}ωn1​, the mode shape is such that the ratio of the amplitude of two masses is equal to the solution of above equation.

Similarly, in either of the equations, substituting for ω\omegaω the value of ωn2\omega_{n2}ωn2​, we have the second mode shape of the system i.e., when the system is vibrating with the second natural frequency ωn2\omega_{n2}ωn2​, the mode shape is such that the ratio of the amplitude of two masses is equal to the solution of above equation.

On solving the equations of motion in polar coordinates and we are going to use the Lagrangian method (L=T−VL=T-VL=T−V where TTT is kinetic energy of system and VVV is potential energy of system) to derive them.

The kinetic energy of the system:

T=12m1v12+12m2v22T = \frac{1}{2} m_1 v_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_2 v_2^2T=21​m1​v12​+21​m2​v22​

T=12m1(x˙12+y˙12)+12m2(x˙22+y˙22)T = \frac{1}{2} m_1 (\dot{x}_1^2 + \dot{y}_1^2) + \frac{1}{2} m_2 (\dot{x}_2^2 + \dot{y}_2^2)T=21​m1​(x˙12​+y˙​12​)+21​m2​(x˙22​+y˙​22​)

T=12m1l12θ˙12+12m2[l12θ˙12+l22θ˙22+2l1l2θ˙1θ˙2cos⁡(θ1−θ2)]T = \frac{1}{2} m_1 l_1^2 \dot{\theta}_1^2 + \frac{1}{2} m_2 \left[ l_1^2 \dot{\theta}_1^2 + l_2^2 \dot{\theta}_2^2 + 2 l_1 l_2 \dot{\theta}_1 \dot{\theta}_2 \cos(\theta_1 - \theta_2) \right]T=21​m1​l12​θ˙12​+21​m2​[l12​θ˙12​+l22​θ˙22​+2l1​l2​θ˙1​θ˙2​cos(θ1​−θ2​)]

The potential energy of system:

V=m1gy1+m2gy2V = m_1 g y_1 + m_2 g y_2V=m1​gy1​+m2​gy2​

V=−(m1+m2)gl1cos⁡θ1−m2gl2cos⁡θ2V = - (m_1 + m_2) g l_1 \cos\theta_1 - m_2 g l_2 \cos\theta_2V=−(m1​+m2​)gl1​cosθ1​−m2​gl2​cosθ2​

The Lagrange equations for θ1\theta_1θ1​ and θ2\theta_2θ2​ are,

ddt(dLdθ˙l)−dLdθl=0\frac{d}{dt} \left( \frac{d L}{d \dot{\theta}_l} \right) - \frac{d L}{d \theta_l} = 0dtd​(dθ˙l​dL​)−dθl​dL​=0

On solving,

θ¨1=g(sin⁡θ2cos⁡(θ1−θ2)−μsin⁡θ1)−(l2θ˙22+l1θ˙12cos⁡(θ1−θ2))sin⁡(θ1−θ2)l1(μ−cos⁡2(θ1−θ2))\ddot{\theta}_1 = \frac{g(\sin\theta_2 \cos(\theta_1 - \theta_2) - \mu \sin\theta_1) - (l_2 \dot{\theta}_2^2 + l_1 \dot{\theta}_1^2 \cos(\theta_1 - \theta_2)) \sin(\theta_1 - \theta_2)}{l_1 (\mu - \cos^2(\theta_1 - \theta_2))}θ¨1​=l1​(μ−cos2(θ1​−θ2​))g(sinθ2​cos(θ1​−θ2​)−μsinθ1​)−(l2​θ˙22​+l1​θ˙12​cos(θ1​−θ2​))sin(θ1​−θ2​)​

θ¨2=gμ(sin⁡θ1cos⁡(θ1−θ2)−sin⁡θ2)−(μl1θ˙12+l2θ˙22cos⁡(θ1−θ2))sin⁡(θ1−θ2)l2(μ−cos⁡2(θ1−θ2))\ddot{\theta}_2 = \frac{g \mu (\sin\theta_1 \cos(\theta_1 - \theta_2) - \sin\theta_2) - (\mu l_1 \dot{\theta}_1^2 + l_2 \dot{\theta}_2^2 \cos(\theta_1 - \theta_2)) \sin(\theta_1 - \theta_2)}{l_2 (\mu - \cos^2(\theta_1 - \theta_2))}θ¨2​=l2​(μ−cos2(θ1​−θ2​))gμ(sinθ1​cos(θ1​−θ2​)−sinθ2​)−(μl1​θ˙12​+l2​θ˙22​cos(θ1​−θ2​))sin(θ1​−θ2​)​

Where μ=1+(m1+m2)\mu = 1 + (m_1 + m_2)μ=1+(m1​+m2​), θ¨1 \ddot θ1 θ¨1 and θ¨2 \ddot θ2 θ¨2 are angular accelerations of point masses m1m_1m1​ and m2m_2m2​ and θ˙1 \dot θ1 θ˙1 and θ˙2 \dot θ2 θ˙2 are angular velocities of point masses m1m_1m1​ and m2m_2m2​.
